About Me

Career Aspirations

Hi I'm Brandon Fu. A current high school senior on his way to college with future aspirations. I have a strong appreciation for modern-day Economics and want to combine that passion with my computer skills.

Economics determine how our society acts on a day-to-day basis. Individuals face a limited supply of resources and must decide how to allocate those resources to address needs.

Society’s increased reliance on computers and technology has driven us to complete tasks a human could never complete. We have managed to automate mundane processes: something that has fascinated me since my initial exposure to computer programming. In essence, computer science is valued among all job prospects and becoming a quantitative analyst combines both economics and computer science. The market opportunity for computer science in all analysis career fields is limitless.

Technology provides the modern quantitative analyst with new tools in increasing their ability to analyze and ultimately act on a wealth of data. Analysts utilize machine learning and artificial intelligence in conjunction with productivity applications such as Excel and Access to expand the reach of their analytical ability. Along with Computer Science and Economics, a quantitative analyst requires knowledge in statistics, mathematics, and finance.

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) states that quantitative analysis is a field projected to grow by at least “16% between 2012 and 2022”. This number is faster than the job growth of all occupations. According to Investopedia, in 2018, a quantitative analyst earns a median salary of $83,684, much greater than the national median salary of $37,040. I hope to hold a career in this field and find that the only barrier for me to join this emerging job-space is: myself. I plan to fully utilize my next four years of post-secondary education to gain enhance my needed skills of this career while exploring ways to acquire hands-on experience.
