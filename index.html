About Me

Career Insight and Aspirations

Hello! My name is Darren Hsing. An incoming college first-year with ambition and future aspirations. I have a strong appreciation for modern-day Finance & Tech and want to combine that passion with my zeal for problem solving and collaboration.

Finance is the management, creation, and study of money and investments. Individuals, organizations, and governments face choices on how to acquire capital and how they will control their expenditures. Finances are commonly broken down into corporate finance, personal finance, and public finance.

The fourth industrial revolution has increased society’s reliance on technology and the power of computation that drives us to achieve what seemed impossible. As technology and artificial intelligence takes over industries by automating mundane processes, having computer science and analytical skills as part of your repertoire becomes more and more valuable in addition to having strong business acumen. The market opportunity for computer science in all analysis career fields is limitless. As businesses and organizations grow with improved technology, they rely on the financial markets to hire, invest, and grow. The demand for job prospects in the financial markets has consistently increased.

Technology provides the modern financial analyst with new tools to evolve their ability to analyze and predict with a wealth of data. Analysts today utilize machine learning and artificial intelligence in tandem with Excel and databases to maximize their analytical ability. Becoming a consultant focused in finance and tech combines both analysis and problem solving. Along with Finance and Tech, the modern consultant/analyst requires knowledge in statistics, mathematics, finance, and management.

The U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) states that quantitative analysis is a field projected to grow by at least “16% between 2019 and 2029”. This number is faster than the job growth of all occupations. The BLS also states that the investment analysis field (consulting) is projected to grow by at least “11% between 2019 and 2029”. According to Investopedia, an average starting consultant with a Bachelor’s Degree earns an average salary between $64,000 and $100,000 while an average starting consultant with a Master’s in Business Administration (MBA) can earn up to $200,000. This is far greater than the national median salary of $37,040. I hope to hold a career in this field and I plan to fully utilize my next four years of post-secondary education to enhance the skills required of this career while exploring unique opportunities to obtain hands-on experience.
